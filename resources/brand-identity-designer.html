The Psychology of Brand Identity

A brand is not just a logo. It is a gut feeling. When you see the color Tiffany Blue, you immediately think of luxury and exclusivity. When you see the Coca-Cola Red, you feel energy and passion. This is not accidental; it is engineered.

2. Typography: The Voice of Your Brand

If color is the emotion, typography is the voice. The font you choose determines how your message is "heard" by the reader.

  • Serif Fonts (e.g., Times New Roman, Playfair): Traditional, respectable, and reliable. Perfect for law firms and luxury brands.
  • Sans-Serif Fonts (e.g., Helvetica, Inter): Modern, clean, and approachable. The standard for tech startups and digital products.
  • Script Fonts: Creative, elegant, and personal. Often used for wedding photography or boutique crafts.

3. Building a Design System

Consistency is the key to branding. A Design System ensures that your brand looks the same on a mobile app, a billboard, and a business card. It acts as a single source of truth for your team.

Start small: define your primary color, your secondary accent, your two core fonts, and your logo usage rules. As you grow, expand this into a comprehensive UI kit with button styles, spacing grids, and tone of voice guidelines.
