The Psychology of Selling

People don't buy products; they buy better versions of themselves. Great copywriting isn't about being clever. It's about clarity, empathy, and psychology.

1. The AIDA Framework

The oldest and most reliable formula in marketing. Used for everything from TV commercials to TikToks.

AIDA Best for Landing Pages
  • Attention: Stop the scroll. (e.g., "Stop drinking poison.")
  • Interest: Engage with facts. (e.g., "Most coffee has mold toxins.")
  • Desire: Show the dream. (e.g., "Imagine waking up fully energized.")
  • Action: Tell them what to do. (e.g., "Click here to buy.")

2. The PAS Framework

Fear is a stronger motivator than gain. PAS leverages this by highlighting the pain before offering relief.

PAS Best for Emails & Ads
  • Problem: Identify the pain. (e.g., "Tired of the 2 PM crash?")
  • Agitation: Twist the knife. (e.g., "It ruins your productivity and mood.")
  • Solution: Save the day. (e.g., "Drink SuperCoffee for sustained focus.")

4. Headline Formulas That Work

David Ogilvy famously said, "On the average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy." If your headline fails, you've wasted your money.

  • The "How To" Headline:
    "How to [Achieve Benefit] Without [Pain Point]"
  • The Listicle:
    "[Number] Ways to [Achieve Benefit] in [Timeframe]"
  • The Secret:
    "The Little-Known Secret to [Desirable Outcome]"

5. Cognitive Biases in Marketing

Great copywriters are amateur psychologists. They understand the glitches in the human brain and use them ethically.

People rely heavily on the first piece of information they see.
Tactic: Show the "Regular Price" ($199) next to the "Sale Price" ($99). The $99 feels like a steal.

6. The "So What?" Test

This is the ultimate editing tool. Read every sentence of your copy and ask "So what?".

Draft 1: "Our phone has a 5000mAh battery." (So what?)

Draft 2: "It lasts 24 hours." (So what?)

Final Copy: "Stop worrying about your charger. Go all day and night on a single charge." (Sold.)
